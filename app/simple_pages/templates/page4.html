Article 4 (SOLID)

The SOLID principles are five principles of OOP design.

  • Single Responsibility Principle
  • Open-closed Principle
  • Liskov Substitution Principle
  • Interface Segregation Principle
  • Dependency Inversion Principle

First, Single Responsibility Principle is essentially a class that only does one specific task and one single reason to change. Second, Open-closed principle states that classes should be open for extension and closed modification. This process allows our program to add functionality without touching the code. In our calculator program, one example of open-closed principle is when establishing a parent to children relationship between classes that allows children to use the properties of its parent class. Third, Liskov Substitution Principle states that subclasses should be suitable for their base classes.

Lastly, Dependency Inversion Principle states that classes should depend upon interfaces or abstract classes instead of concrete classes and functions.
